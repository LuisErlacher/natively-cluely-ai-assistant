--- Raw model answer ---
## Likely Cause
A production streaming pipeline issue, most often caused by **consumer lag or backpressure**,  Kafka consumers can't keep up with the ingest rate because of slow downstream processing, partition skew, or a stuck/deserializing record blocking the consumer thread.

## How I Would Investigate
- Check consumer lag per partition (`kafka-consumer-groups.sh --describe`) to see if lag is concentrated on one partition.
- Inspect consumer thread metrics: poll time vs. processing time, GC pauses, JVM heap.
- Look for **poison pills**, malformed records causing repeated deserialization failures.
- Check downstream sinks: DB connection pool saturation, slow flushes, network timeouts.
- Confirm partition count matches key cardinality, since a hot key can crush one partition.

## Fix
- Increase partition count and rebalance consumer instances to parallelize load.
- Switch to **manual commits** so a bad record doesn't block the offset; route poison pills to a dead-letter topic.
- Tune `max.poll.records`, `fetch.min.bytes`, and consumer JVM heap.
- For Flink: enable checkpointing, tune `buffer-timeout`, and increase TaskManager parallelism for the skewed operator.

## Validation
- Watch lag drain to near-zero within a healthy processing window.
- Confirm throughput (events/sec) matches or exceeds peak ingest.
- Re-run with an injected poison pill and verify it lands in the DLQ without halting the pipeline.

## Prevention
- Schema validation at ingest with a schema registry and explicit `DeserializationException` handling.
- Alerting on consumer lag, GC time, and processing duration per partition.
- Load-test against 2, 3x peak before promoting changes.
- Regular partition-key audits to catch skew early.
